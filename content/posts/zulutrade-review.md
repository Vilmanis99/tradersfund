<p>ZuluGuard is like a safety switch for your account. It watches the traders (Leaders) you’re copying and steps in if things start to go wrong.</p>

<p>Sometimes, a Leader might suddenly change their trading style or take bigger risks than usual. If that happens, ZuluGuard can spot it. Based on the protection settings <em>you</em> choose, it will close all trades from that Leader and stop copying them before things get worse.</p>

<p>In short, ZuluGuard helps protect your money by acting fast when a Leader's strategy starts going off track.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">ZuluTrade Review on Its Journey&nbsp;</h2>

<p>To really get the full picture in this ZuluTrade review, it's helpful to take a quick look at how the platform has grown over time. They’ve definitely come a long way! Here’s a simple breakdown of their journey</p>

<figure class="wp-block-gallery has-nested-images columns-default is-cropped">
<figure class="wp-block-image size-large rounded-img"><img src="/images/wp/2025/07/Screenshot-2025-07-10-010547.jpg" alt="ZuluTrade Journey Overview" class="wp-image-2209"/></figure>
</figure>

<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>2007</strong>: ZuluTrade was started by Leon Yohai and Kosta Eleftheriou in Virginia, USA. It became the first platform that let people copy trades without being tied to one broker.</li>

<li><strong>2008</strong>: The platform entered its test phase and added FXCM’s API, which made automated and <a href="/blog/what-is-copy-trading">copy trading</a> possible.</li>

<li><strong>2009</strong>: More traders joined; over 4,500 people were already sharing their strategies.</li>

<li><strong>2011</strong>: They added social features like following other followers and started paying successful traders.</li>

<li><strong>2012–2013</strong>: The website got a new look, and ZuluGuard was launched to help protect users from bad trading results.</li>

<li><strong>2014</strong>: ZuluTrade expanded into Asia by buying a Japanese company and supporting local brokers. The team grew to 120 employees.</li>

<li><strong>2015</strong>: They received an EU license for portfolio management, which helped boost trust in the platform.</li>

<li><strong>2017</strong>: Crypto trading was added, letting users copy crypto strategies too.</li>

<li><strong>2020</strong>: ZuluTrade added stock CFDs and started offering services in the U.S. under local rules.</li>

<li><strong>December 2021</strong>: The platform was bought by Finvasia Group, which helped it grow even more.</li>

<li><strong>2022–2023</strong>: They added stricter rules for brokers, removed profit-sharing fees for investors, got new licenses in South Africa and Mauritius, won some industry awards, and improved social trading features.</li>

<li><strong>Today</strong>: As this ZuluTrade review shows, the platform now works in over 150 countries, with 30 million accounts and more than 2 million active traders (Leaders). It’s one of the biggest names in copy trading today.</li>
</ul>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Zulutrade Broker Integration</h2>

<p>In the previous part of this ZuluTrade review, we went over the brokers supported directly by the platform. But don’t worry! It doesn’t mean you <em>have</em> to stick with just those. Zulutrade actually supports three different types of brokers, giving you some flexibility based on your preferences.</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Integrated Brokers</h3>

<p>These brokers are fully built into the Zulutrade platform. That means you can connect and manage your trading account directly through Zulutrade without needing to switch between websites.</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Setup is super easy and automatic.</li>

<li>You don’t pay any copy-trading fees to Zulutrade.</li>

<li>A great example is AAAFx, which is actually Zulutrade’s own broker.</li>
</ul>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Co-Branded Brokers</h3>

<p>These are brokers that have a special partnership with Zulutrade and even have a dedicated Zulutrade-branded area for their users.</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>You’ll need to open an account with the broker separately, then link it to Zulutrade.</li>

<li>They have a partial connection to Zulutrade.</li>

<li>Some might offer perks like lower fees or bonus deals.</li>

<li>You <em>might</em> pay a small fee (like $10/month per strategy), or nothing at all if the broker covers it.</li>
</ul>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Standard Brokers</h3>

<p>These are any brokers that support platforms like MT4, MT5, ActTrader, or XOH. You can use your account from almost anywhere in the world (just make sure it’s allowed in your region).</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>These brokers aren’t directly connected to Zulutrade.</li>

<li>You’ll still be able to use them by linking your trading account manually through MT4/MT5.</li>

<li>In this case, Zulutrade charges a small fee, $10/month for each strategy you follow.</li>
</ul>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">ZuluTrader Investors Account Review</h2>

<p>Well, as I mentioned at the beginning of this ZuluTrade review, the platform is designed to suit a wide range of trading goals; whether you're an investor looking to copy trades or a pro trader wanting to share your own strategies.</p>

<p>If you’re an Investor, your main goal is to follow and copy trades from professional traders (called “Leaders”). Remember when I talked about the simulation feature that lets you test out a trader’s strategy before committing? That’s done using a demo account. But if you're ready to go live, you’ve got two types of real accounts to choose from:</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">1. Classic Account</h3>

<p>With the Classic Account:</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>You select  strategies to copy.</li>

<li>You can manually adjust, close, or manage trades whenever you like.</li>

<li>ZuluTrade doesn’t charge a performance fee.</li>

<li>If you're using an Integrated Broker, you pay $0 in copy-trading fees.</li>

<li>If you're with a Standard Broker, there’s a $10/month fee per strategy.</li>
</ul>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">2. Profit Sharing Account</h3>

<p>The second option is the Profit Sharing Account, which works a bit differently:</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>ZuluTrade manages and executes trades automatically for you.</li>

<li>You don’t have manual control here! Everything is automated.</li>

<li>Before 2023, there was a $30/month charge plus a 25% performance fee.</li>

<li>Since January 2023, it's completely free, with no charges from ZuluTrade.</li>

<li>This account type is only available through select brokers (mainly Integrated and Co-Branded ones).</li>
</ul>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">ZuluTrader Leaders Accounts Review</h2>

<p>Just like investors, Leaders on ZuluTrade also have two types of accounts: Classic and Profit Sharing. If you’re a Leader, your goal is to share your trading strategies so others can copy them, and you get paid for your performance.</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">1. Classic Leader Account</h3>

<p>With a Classic Leader account:</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>You connect your existing broker account (usually MT4 or MT5).</li>

<li>Your trades are shared in real-time with your followers.</li>

<li>You earn commissions based on the trading volume that’s copied from you.</li>

<li>It’s a great option if you already use MT4/MT5 and want more flexibility.</li>
</ul>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">2. Profit Sharing Leader Account</h3>

<p>With a Profit Sharing Leader account:</p>

<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>You trade directly through ZuluTrade’s system or connect a supported broker account. (I shared the supported broker list above in this ZuluTrade Review)</li>

<li>Your earnings are based on the net profits you generate for your followers.</li>

<li>You need to be approved first. ZuluTrade checks your performance, <a href="/blog/what-is-prop-firm-consistency-rule">consistency</a>, and risk level.</li>

<li>Since January 2023, payments have come from ZuluTrade itself, not directly from followers.</li>
</ul>
