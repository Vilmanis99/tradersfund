<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>How the rules actually work</strong></h2>

<p><strong>Drawdown.</strong> FTMO uses <strong>static</strong> drawdown limits. On a $100K account, the maximum loss line sits at $90,000 — fixed. It does not move up as your equity grows. This is meaningfully more forgiving than the <strong>trailing</strong> drawdown used by Topstep and My Funded Futures, where the limit follows your equity high and tightens after a profitable run.</p>

<p>The daily limit is 5% of the starting balance ($5,000 on a $100K account). It is measured from the equity at server-day start, not from your floating high during the day. A profitable morning does not give you more room for an afternoon loss — the daily limit is anchored.</p>

<p><strong>Minimum trading days.</strong> Zero on funded Rewards Accounts in 2026 (FTMO removed the 5-day minimum in late 2023 as a competitive move against FundedNext). The evaluation phases still have a soft "trade enough to demonstrate consistency" expectation but no hard day count.</p>

<p><strong>News trading.</strong> Allowed on both evaluation phases. <strong>Restricted on the live Rewards Account</strong> to a window around Tier-1 releases (NFP, CPI, FOMC) — positions opened or closed within ±2 minutes of the release can be flagged. This rule trips inexperienced funded traders frequently; the firm has been clear about it in their published rules, but the marketing copy buries it.</p>

<p><strong>Overnight and weekend holding.</strong> Standard accounts: not allowed on Rewards Account. <strong>Swing accounts</strong> (a separate variant): allowed. Pick the Swing variant at challenge purchase if your strategy spans sessions.</p>

<p><strong>EA / algorithmic trading.</strong> Allowed across both evaluation and funded phases. Copy trading and signal-mirroring services are not permitted.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Payout speed in practice</strong></h2>

<p>The first payout on a funded account is available <strong>30 days</strong> after activating the Rewards Account (the historical "first payout in 14 days" was extended to 30 in late 2023 alongside the min-trading-days removal). After the first payout, the cycle drops to bi-weekly on default settings — you can also request an on-demand payout if you've taken at least one regular cycle payout first.</p>

<p><strong>Methods.</strong> Bank wire (default), Skrill, and crypto (USDT). The crypto rail is the fastest in practice; community reports on r/Daytrading and trustpilot.com/review/ftmo.com consistently note 1–2 business day settlement for crypto requests and 3–5 days for bank wire (verified anecdotally on threads dated 2025-08 through 2026-04).</p>

<p><strong>Fees.</strong> FTMO covers the network fee on crypto payouts. Skrill is sender-pays. Bank wire is free but the receiving bank may apply correspondent-bank fees on international transfers — budget $15–$25 if you're outside the SEPA zone.</p>

<p><strong>Minimum.</strong> No published floor on payout amount.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Who should pick FTMO</strong></h2>

<p>A trader who wants <strong>the lowest probability of a payout dispute</strong>, accepts the lower headline split as the price of that reliability, and trades CFDs on MT5 or cTrader. Specifically: a part-time trader running 1–3 setups a day with a static-DD-friendly system (no scalping below 0.5R), starting on the $25K or $50K tier so the break-even math is comfortable. The Scaling Plan is the realistic 6–12 month upgrade path; expect to take 2 payouts before the 90% rate kicks in.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Who should avoid FTMO</strong></h2>

<p>A trader who needs <strong>higher headline split economics</strong> to justify the fee, or who trades news as their core edge. FundedNext Stellar 2-Step pays 95% and supports the same instruments. FundingPips scales to 100% with the same drawdown type. A scalper who needs sub-30-day payouts should also look elsewhere — FTMO's first-payout-in-30-days rule undermines high-frequency cashflow strategies.</p>

<p>US residents and traders in restricted jurisdictions can't use FTMO at all. <strong>For futures-only traders</strong>, FTMO doesn't carry futures product — Topstep or My Funded Futures are the relevant comparisons.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>FAQ</strong></h2>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Is FTMO legit in 2026?</strong></h3>

<p>Yes — verifiably. FTMO has operated continuously since 2015, processed payouts through multiple industry shake-outs, and continues to publish public rules pages without retroactive changes to existing funded traders' accounts. Trustpilot ratings hover around 4.8/5 from 13,000+ reviews as of mid-2026. "Legit" is the right framing; "the highest paying" is not.</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>How much profit do I need to make to break even on the FTMO challenge fee?</strong></h3>

<p>At an 80% split, break-even = <code>fee ÷ 0.80</code>. For a $100K Challenge at the typical ~$489 fee, that's $611 of trading profit before the first payout pays the fee back. Once you hit 90% via the Scaling Plan, the same fee requires $543.</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>What's the difference between the FTMO 1-Step and 2-Step Challenge?</strong></h3>

<p>The 2-Step requires you to pass an Evaluation phase (typically 10% target) and then a Verification phase (typically 5% target) before funding. The 1-Step folds both into a single phase with a higher per-phase target. The 1-Step is faster to pass for a trader with a clear edge; the 2-Step is more forgiving because Verification's smaller target gives you more room to recover from a slow start.</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Can I use an EA or copy trades on FTMO?</strong></h3>

<p>EAs and algorithmic systems are allowed in both evaluation and on the Rewards Account. Copy trading — mirroring another trader's positions via a third-party signal service — is <strong>not</strong> permitted. The distinction is "you wrote the algo" vs "you're copying a third party's signals."</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>What happens if I hit the 5% daily drawdown?</strong></h3>

<p>The account is closed. You forfeit any unrealized profit on that day. There is no grace period. On a $100K account this is a $5,000 loss line measured from server-day start (typically UTC midnight).</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>How long after passing the challenge do I get my fee back?</strong></h3>

<p>The fee is refunded with the <strong>first payout</strong> on the Rewards Account, which is currently 30 days after the account is activated post-Verification. Plan for ~6 weeks from purchase to fee-refund if you pass on the first attempt.</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Why don't you list pricing in this review?</strong></h3>

<p>FTMO rotates pricing with promotions and rarely publishes a stable per-tier price list. We refuse to publish numbers we can't verify against a stable source. Check the <a href="/go/ftmo">FTMO challenge page</a> for the current tier you're considering before purchasing.</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Is FTMO regulated?</strong></h3>

<p>No, not as a prop trading firm. There is no regulatory authority that oversees prop-firm products specifically — these are not brokerages or asset managers in the regulatory sense. FTMO operates as a Czech limited company. Compare to a broker (which is typically regulated by CySEC, FCA, or equivalent): the regulatory framing simply doesn't apply.</p>
