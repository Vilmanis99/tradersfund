<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>How the rules actually work</strong></h2>

<p><strong>Static drawdown</strong> — forgiving structure shared with FTMO, FundedNext, FundingPips. Loss line doesn't tighten on profit. On a $25K Bright Funded account, the max-loss line is fixed at $22,500 — a $2,000 winning day to equity $27,000 does not push the floor up to $24,300 (which is what trailing-DD firms do). This is the structural reason traders who give back unrealised PnL during a profitable run survive on Bright Funded but fail on Topstep / MFF.</p>

<p>Daily loss caps not on accessible public pages — industry-typical levels (3–5% daily) likely apply; verify on the live signup flow before sizing the first trade.</p>

<p><strong>EA / algorithmic trading allowed</strong> on MT5. TradeLocker EA support is more limited than MT5 by design.</p>

<p><strong>Overnight holding allowed</strong>; weekend holding not confirmed in our data — verify directly.</p>

<p><strong>News trading rules</strong> aren't documented in accessible public sources; treat as restricted until confirmed.</p>

<p><strong>Scaling plan</strong> available for funded traders — exact tier milestones not publicly verified.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Payout speed in practice</strong></h2>

<p>Community reports on Trustpilot are positive but low-volume — Bright Funded sits below the major firms in review count, so individual experience variance is higher.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Who should pick Bright Funded</strong></h2>

<p>A trader specifically committed to TradeLocker, testing a strategy at $10K–$50K account sizes, comfortable with monthly payout cadence. Useful as a low-cost validation firm — but not the right home for traders who'll graduate beyond $400K of allocation or need faster cashflow.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Who should avoid Bright Funded</strong></h2>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>FAQ</strong></h2>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Is Bright Funded legit in 2026?</strong></h3>
<p>Yes — operating since 2023, no major documented disputes. Trustpilot ratings are positive but on lower review volume than the major firms.</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Why monthly payouts instead of bi-weekly?</strong></h3>
<p>Operational choice by the firm. Monthly cadence is below the 2026 industry standard of bi-weekly. For traders who treat payouts as quarterly bonuses this isn't a problem; for traders relying on weekly cashflow, it's a real disadvantage vs faster-cycle competitors.</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>What's special about TradeLocker?</strong></h3>
<p>TradeLocker is a newer trading platform designed to compete with MT4/MT5. It has a cleaner UI and modern API. Few major prop firms support it — Bright Funded does, which matters if you've already built workflow around the platform.</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Can I scale beyond $400K on Bright Funded?</strong></h3>
<p>Their scaling plan exists but caps at $400K total per their published structure. Traders who plan to scale beyond should consider a firm with a higher ceiling (<a href="/blog/ftmo-review">FTMO at $2M</a>, <a href="/blog/fundednext-review">FundedNext at $4M</a>, <a href="/blog/ofp-funding-review">OFP Funding at $5M</a>).</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Can US residents use Bright Funded?</strong></h3>
<p>No. Like most CFD prop firms, Bright Funded doesn't accept US residents due to CFTC restrictions.</p>
